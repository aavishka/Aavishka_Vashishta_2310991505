1.Book Name:Children of Anguish and Anarchy
Book Author:Tomi Adeyemi

Review:"Children of Anguish and Anarchy" by Brian McClellan is a fantasy novel. It features a magical world, complex characters, and intense battles. Fans appreciate the gripping plot and deep character development. It's part of the "Gods of Blood and Powder" series, known for its detailed world-building.
Rating:4/5( ★ ★ ★ ★ ☆)


2.Book Name: Unfinished
Book Author: Priyanka Chopra Jonas

Review:"Unfinished" by Priyanka Chopra is a memoir that delves into her life, from her childhood in India to her rise in Bollywood and Hollywood. Chopra shares personal anecdotes, struggles, and triumphs, highlighting her journey as an actress, producer, and global icon. The book is candid, inspiring, and reflective.
Rating:4/5( ★ ★ ★ ★ ☆)


3.Book Name: Rich Dad Poor Dad
Book Author: Robert T.Kiyosaki

Review:"Rich Dad Poor Dad" by Robert Kiyosaki contrasts his two father figures: his biological father (poor dad) and his best friend's father (rich dad). It teaches financial literacy, stressing the importance of investing, understanding assets and liabilities, and promoting financial independence through entrepreneurship and smart investing.
Rating:5/5( ★ ★ ★ ★ ★)


4.Book Name:The Simple Wild
Book Author: K.A Tucker

Review:The Simple World is a straightforward, engaging book. It explores basic, clear concepts about our world. Easy to read, it appeals to all ages. The author presents ideas simply and effectively.
Rating:3/5( ★ ★ ★ ☆ ☆)


5.Book Name:IKIGAI
Book Author: Héctor García and Francesc Miralles

Review:"Ikigai" by Héctor García and Francesc Miralles explores the Japanese concept of finding purpose. It blends philosophy and lifestyle tips, emphasizing balance. The book encourages a fulfilling life through passion, mission, vocation, and profession. It's insightful, motivating, and easy to read.
Rating:4/5( ★ ★ ★ ★ ☆)


6.Book Name:Gita Wisdom Through Quotes
Book Author: Chaitanya Charan

Review:"Gita Wisdom Through Codes" simplifies Bhagavad Gita teachings using modern coding analogies. It's concise, insightful, and bridges spiritual concepts with technology. The book is accessible for beginners and offers a fresh perspective on ancient wisdom, making it relevant and engaging for today's readers.
Rating:4/5( ★ ★ ★ ★ ☆)


7.Book Name:50 Great Short Stories
Book Author: Milton Crane

Review:"50 Great Short Stories" is a classic collection. Each story is unique, showcasing various styles and themes. Edited by Milton Crane, it features famous authors like Hemingway and Poe. The book is perfect for short reading sessions, offering timeless, diverse tales. Highly recommended for lovers of literature.
Rating:5/5( ★ ★ ★ ★ ★)


8.Book Name: Think Like a Monk
Book Author: Jay Shetty

Review:"Think Like a Monk" by Jay Shetty teaches practical wisdom from monk life for modern challenges. It covers gratitude, self-discipline, and mindfulness. Shetty offers personal stories and actionable advice to improve mental clarity, relationships, and personal growth.
Rating:5/5( ★ ★ ★ ★ ★)


9.Book Name: It starts with Us
Book Author:Colleen Hoover

Review:"It Starts with Us" by Colleen Hoover is a sequel to "It Ends with Us." It focuses on Lily and Atlas's rekindled romance. The book explores themes of love, healing, and second chances. The continuation of the story is fascinating.
Rating:4/5( ★ ★ ★ ★ ☆)


10.Book Name: Eat That Frog!
Book Author: Brian Tracy

Review:"Eat That Frog" by Brian Tracy is a productivity book offering 21 tips to overcome procrastination. The main idea is to tackle the most challenging task first ("eating the frog") to improve efficiency. It's practical, straightforward, and emphasizes time management and goal setting.
Rating:4/5( ★ ★ ★ ★ ☆)


11.Book Name:Ghost Stories
Book Author: Charles Dickens

Review:"Ghost Stories by Charles Dickens" showcases his mastery in crafting eerie tales, blending supernatural elements with human emotions. Dickens weaves suspense and chilling atmosphere effortlessly, making each story hauntingly memorable. His characters encounter ghosts that reflect societal issues, adding depth to the supernatural. It's a classic collection for any ghost story enthusiast.
Rating:4/5( ★ ★ ★ ★ ☆)


12.Book Name:Asterix Omnibus
Book Author: Alberto Aleandro Uderzo

Review:"Asterix Omnibus" compiles classic comics by René Goscinny and Albert Uderzo, blending humor with historical satire. Set in ancient Gaul, it follows Asterix and Obelix's adventures against Romans. Clever wordplay, vibrant artwork, and cultural references make it timeless. Perfect for all ages, it's a joyous journey through wit and whimsy.
Rating:3/5( ★ ★ ★ ☆ ☆)
